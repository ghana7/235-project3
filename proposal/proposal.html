High Concept

Original Idea:

An idle clicker game based around plants.

Then slightly refined:

An incremental farming game where you grow and combine crops to make profits.

Genre

Incremental Game / Farming Sim

At its core, this game is an incremental game, which means it starts with small, simple mechanics, and grows to become more complex and interesting.

The growth will come through different farming mechanics, crop combinations, and economic strategies.

Platform

Web-based game

This game will be built for web play, using PixiJS.

Story

While wandering through the forests, you run into an abandoned farm with pictures of some weird-looking crops on its signposts. You only see a few seeds, though, and decide to begin to learn plant hybridization to discover the secrets of the farm (and make a profit).

Aesthetics

The graphics style will be old-timey, like an old, dirty farm. Sprites for plants, seeds, and other various objects will be pixel graphics, in a similar old, faded style.

Gameplay

Mechanics

The player has a field of crops that grow over time. As crops are harvested, they can either be sold for money or combined to create new crops. Money can be spent on various upgrades, such as field explansions, fertilizer, or machines that will help the player farm more efficiently.

Control

Most action, such as planting crops and purchasing, will happen with the mouse, but hotkeys could be a potential addition to enhance the user experience

Onboarding

Since this is an incremental game, the entire game is just a long onboarding process. The player will start with only a few very cheap, fast growing crops. They will quickly be able to experiment with hybrids of these crops and the purchase of upgrades, all tempered by their money income, which will limit the overwhelming nature of endless possibilites.

Player learning

Players will improve at the game by recognizing new ways to combine crops, knowing which upgrades to buy, and becoming more efficient at managing resources.
